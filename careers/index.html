Common questions

Frequently asked

Do I need experience?

No. Most of our carers started with no care experience. We provide full paid training (Care Certificate) in your first week. What matters most is kindness, reliability and a willingness to learn.

What driving licence do I need?

You don't need a driving licence to be a Hayat carer — many of our clients are on public transport routes, and we cluster visits geographically so you can walk between them. A driving licence is helpful for live-in or rural posts, but not required.

How much will I earn?

Hourly rates are well above the National Living Wage, reviewed every 6 months. Travel time between visits is paid, mileage is reimbursed at HMRC rates, and you get 28 days paid holiday (pro-rata) plus a workplace pension. We'll share the exact rate band for your role at interview.

Can I work school hours only?

Yes — many of our carers work 9am–3pm, term-time only, and the rota is built around the school run. We design schedules around your life, not the other way round.

What does the training cover?

Your first week covers the Care Certificate (mandatory UK training): safeguarding, manual handling, medication, first aid, dementia awareness, and equality & diversity. We then fund Level 2 and Level 3 Diplomas in Health & Social Care, plus specialist modules (end-of-life, Parkinson's, cultural competency). All training is paid.

Are you zero-hours?

No. All Hayat carers are employed directly with guaranteed hours contracts (full or part-time). You'll always know your rota at least 2 weeks in advance, and you'll have consistent client visits — not random one-offs.
