The programme

Three stages, fully funded, paid as you learn

You earn a salary from week one. We pay for the training, the exams, and your time in the classroom. You turn up, learn, and qualify.

Stage 1
🌱

Care Certificate · Week 1

One week of paid classroom-based training covering the fundamentals: safeguarding, manual handling, medication awareness, first aid, dementia basics, and equality & diversity.

  • 5 days, fully paid
  • In-person at our Birmingham training centre
  • Mix of theory + practical
  • Certificate on completion
Stage 2
📚

Level 2 Diploma · Months 1–6

The nationally-recognised qualification for adult care workers. Delivered on-the-job with monthly assessor visits. Covers person-centred care, safeguarding, communication, health & safety.

  • Fully funded by Hayat Care
  • On-the-job learning + monthly workshops
  • 1-to-1 assessor support
  • Diploma on completion
Stage 3
🚀

Level 3 Diploma · Months 6–18

For those ready to step up into senior carer or team-lead roles. Optional but fully funded. Covers advanced care planning, clinical skills, leadership & supervision.

  • Fully funded by Hayat Care
  • Recognised by NHS & social services
  • Pathway to senior roles
  • Diploma on completion

Common questions

About the training programme

Is the training really 100% free?

Yes. Hayat Care funds the full cost of the Care Certificate, Level 2 Diploma, and (if you choose to progress) the Level 3 Diploma. There are no fees, no loans, no hidden costs. We also pay your salary while you're in training — even the classroom week.

Do I need qualifications to start?

No formal qualifications are required. You need the right to work in the UK, conversational English, and a DBS check (which we pay for and process). If you can read this page, you can start.

How long is the training?

The Care Certificate takes 1 week of paid classroom time. The Level 2 Diploma typically takes 6 months part-time alongside your normal shifts. The Level 3 Diploma is another 6–12 months. You can stop at any stage — you don't have to do all three.

Where does the training take place?

The Care Certificate is delivered at our Birmingham training centre. Diplomas are a mix of monthly in-person workshops and on-the-job learning with assessor visits. We cluster training days to minimise disruption to your rota.

What if I fail an exam?

You can resit — no penalty, no judgement. Our pass rate is 96% on first attempt, and 100% by second attempt. If you struggle, your mentor and assessor will give you extra support before any resit.

Is the qualification recognised elsewhere?

Yes. The Care Certificate is the UK-wide mandatory standard. Level 2 and Level 3 Diplomas in Health & Social Care are recognised by the NHS, social services, and care providers across the country — so your qualification travels with you.
